What will the cyberpunk city be like in the future?

As Kitchin said in her article "Cities in the New Millennium: A View from Science Fiction”,1999


In Cyberpunk... there is usually a "system" that dominates the lives of most “ordinary” people, be it an oppressive government, a group of large, paternalistic corporations, or a fundamentalist religion. These systems are enhanced by… “information technology" (computers, the mass media), making the system better at keeping those inside it.



However, as in any cultural system, there are always those who live on its margins, on "the Edge": criminals, outcasts, visionaries, or those who simply want freedom for its own sake. Cyberpunk literature focuses on these people and how they turn the system's technological tools to their own ends. This is the "punk" aspect of Cyberpunk.

I have always seen cyberpunk cities in science fiction and films about cyberpunk cities. And I think it is a great way to have a vision of cyberpunk to see the reality and social structure in the future.

  • What are the main features of a cyberpunk city? How do they differ from a conventional city?
  • What are the advantages and disadvantages of living in a cyberpunk city? How do people cope with the challenges and opportunities of a high-tech and low-life environment?
  • What are the social and political issues that affect a cyberpunk city? How do corporations, hackers, and artificial intelligence influence the city’s governance and culture?
  • What are the ethical and moral dilemmas that arise in a cyberpunk city? How do people deal with the consequences of cybernetics, biotechnology, and virtual reality?
  • What are the aesthetic and artistic aspects of a cyberpunk city? How do people express their creativity and identity in a cyberpunk city?
